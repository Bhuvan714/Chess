THE PIECES

The King

The king is the piece you protect. It is the most important piece because you will lose without it. You win by making the king unable to move to any spot or stay where it is. The king can only move to adjacenty squares.

The Queen

The queen is the most powerful piece due to its movement. It can move in any horizontal, vertical, or diagonal manner. It is also very easy to checmkmate with and can be big problem if used with many threats on its own.

The Rook

The rook is a piece that moves in straight lines. It can basically switch places with the king by castling. It is a great defender in the beginning of the game and is much more powerful in the endgame due to the open space everywhere.



The Bishop

The bishop is a piece that can olnly move in diagonals. This means that it can only go to half of the board and thus the other bishop has the other side of the board. The bishop is however a very good helper in offense in the beginning.

The Knight

The knight is a peculiar piece that can only move in L shapes. Thus, it has limited range and to compensate for that, it can jump over other pieces. It is also very good at many strategies in the beginning of a game by forks.

The Pawn

The pawn is a staple of the game and there are 8 of them. They move straight 1 square unless it is there first move in which they could choose to move 2 moves. They also capture diagonially, and thus can be used for many situations and formations.
